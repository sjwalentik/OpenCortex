# Contributing to OpenCortex

Thanks for your interest in OpenCortex.

OpenCortex is currently in an early build phase. To preserve product direction, licensing flexibility, and architectural consistency, outside code contributions are intentionally limited.

## Current Contribution Policy

- Please open issues for bugs, ideas, and design discussion before submitting code
- Do not begin large code changes without prior maintainer approval
- Maintainers may decline contributions that do not fit the current roadmap

## Intellectual Property

By submitting any contribution to OpenCortex, including code, documentation, tests, assets, or other materials, you agree that:

- your contribution is original or that you have the legal right to submit it
- you have the authority to grant the rights described here
- you assign all right, title, and interest in and to that contribution, including all associated intellectual property rights, to the OpenCortex project owner
- the project owner may use, relicense, modify, sublicense, distribute, or commercialize that contribution in any form
- your contribution may be accepted, modified, or rejected at the sole discretion of the maintainers

If you do not agree to these terms, do not submit a contribution.

## License Of Contributions

Unless explicitly agreed otherwise in writing by the project owner, all accepted contributions are also distributed as part of the project under the repository's current license.

## No Warranty

By contributing, you represent that your submission does not knowingly include confidential information, trade secrets, or material that would create legal obligations for the project owner.

## Recommended First Step

If you want to contribute, start by opening an issue describing the change you want to make and why it belongs in OpenCortex.
